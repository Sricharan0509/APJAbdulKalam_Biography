About The Legend

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, also known as Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was a renowned Indian scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Here are some of his notable achievements and biographical details

Biographical Facts

Early Life: A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, to a humble Tamil Muslim family with four siblings.

Education: Kalam received both his Bachelor's and Master's degrees in aeronautical and aerospace engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1954 and later.

Family: Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam came from a humble and religious Muslim family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. His father, Jainulabdeen, owned a boat and served as an imam at a nearby mosque, while his mother, Ashiamma, was a homemaker. Despite their financial limitations, Kalam's parents instilled in him a deep-rooted appreciation for values, discipline, and education, which were crucial in shaping his extraordinary life and career as a scientist and politician.

Role in ISRO: Abdul Kalam joined ISRO in 1969 and played a key role in developing India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III.

Missile Development: Dr. Kalam was instrumental in India's missile development program. He served as the Project Director for India's initial satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and subsequently held the position of Chief Executive for the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP). During his tenure, India successfully created the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

Awards and honors: He was awarded the Padma Bhushan in 1981, Padma Vibhushan in 1990, and Bharat Ratna in 1997, India's highest civilian award. He also received several international awards.

Authorship: Dr. Kalam was a prolific author. He wrote several books, including "Wings of Fire," his autobiography, which provides insight into his early life and career. His books, particularly "Ignited Minds," inspired millions of young Indians.

Presidency: In 2002, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India, serving until 2007. He was the first scientist and bachelor to occupy the office of the President.
